MOTOR VEHICLES ACT, 1988 & CENTRAL MOTOR VEHICLES RULES
Traffic Laws, Fines & Penalties in India
[Amended by Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019]

=== PART I: TRAFFIC VIOLATIONS & FINES (POST 2019 AMENDMENT) ===

Section 177 - General Offences (Violation of any provision of the Act)
Fine: Rs. 500 (first offence), Rs. 1,500 (subsequent offence)

Section 177A - Violation of road regulations
Fine: Rs. 500 (first offence), Rs. 1,500 (subsequent offence)

Section 178 - Travelling without ticket
Fine: Rs. 200 plus the fare payable

Section 179 - Disobedience of orders, obstruction and refusal of information
Fine: Rs. 2,000 or imprisonment up to 6 months, or both

Section 180 - Allowing unauthorised persons to drive vehicles
Fine: Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both

Section 181 - Driving without licence
Fine: Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both
Note: Driving without a valid driving licence is a serious offence. Police can seize the vehicle.

Section 182 - Driving despite disqualification
Fine: Rs. 10,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both

Section 182A - Driving vehicle not complying with standards
Fine: Rs. 1,000 (first offence), Rs. 2,000 (subsequent offence)

Section 182B - Driving overloaded vehicle
Fine: Rs. 20,000 plus Rs. 2,000 per tonne of excess load

Section 183 - Driving at excessive speed (Overspeeding)
Light Motor Vehicle: Fine Rs. 1,000 (first offence), Rs. 2,000 (subsequent offence)
Medium Passenger/Goods Vehicle: Fine Rs. 2,000 (first offence), Rs. 4,000 (subsequent offence)
Note: Overspeeding in school zones or near hospitals attracts double penalty.

Section 184 - Dangerous driving
Fine: Rs. 5,000 (first offence) or imprisonment up to 6 months
Subsequent offence: Fine Rs. 10,000 or imprisonment up to 2 years, or both

Section 185 - Driving by a drunken person or by a person under the influence of drugs
Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) exceeding 30mg per 100ml of blood:
Fine: Rs. 10,000 (first offence) or imprisonment up to 6 months
Subsequent offence within 3 years: Fine Rs. 15,000 or imprisonment up to 2 years, or both
Note: Police can conduct breathalyzer test. Refusal to take test is also an offence.

Section 186 - Driving when mentally or physically unfit to drive
Fine: Rs. 1,000 (first offence), Rs. 2,000 (subsequent offence)

Section 187 - Punishment for offences relating to accident
Failure to report accident: Fine Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both

Section 188 - Punishment for abetment of certain offences
Same punishment as the principal offender

Section 189 - Racing and trials of speed on public roads
Fine: Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both

Section 190 - Using vehicle in unsafe condition
Fine: Rs. 1,000 (first offence), Rs. 2,000 (subsequent offence)
If vehicle causes accident: Fine Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both

Section 192 - Using vehicle without registration
Fine: Rs. 5,000 (first offence), Rs. 10,000 (subsequent offence)
Note: Driving an unregistered vehicle is illegal. Vehicle can be seized.

Section 192A - Using vehicle without permit
Fine: Rs. 10,000 (first offence), Rs. 25,000 (subsequent offence)

Section 193 - Punishment of agents and canvassers without proper authority
Fine: Rs. 1,000 (first offence), Rs. 2,000 (subsequent offence)

Section 194 - Driving vehicle exceeding permissible weight (Overloading)
Fine: Rs. 20,000 plus Rs. 2,000 per tonne of excess load
Passenger overloading: Rs. 1,000 per extra passenger

Section 194A - Overloading of passengers
Fine: Rs. 1,000 per extra passenger

Section 194B - Not wearing seat belt
Fine: Rs. 1,000
Note: Seat belt is mandatory for driver and all passengers in front and rear seats.

Section 194C - Not wearing helmet
Fine: Rs. 1,000 and disqualification from holding driving licence for 3 months
Note: Helmet is mandatory for rider and pillion rider on two-wheelers.

Section 194D - Obstruction to free flow of traffic
Fine: Rs. 1,000

Section 194E - Not giving way to emergency vehicles
Fine: Rs. 10,000 or imprisonment up to 6 months, or both
Note: Ambulance, fire brigade, police vehicles must be given way immediately.

Section 194F - Use of mobile phone while driving
Fine: Rs. 5,000
Note: Using handheld mobile phone while driving is strictly prohibited.

Section 196 - Driving uninsured vehicle
Fine: Rs. 2,000 (first offence) or imprisonment up to 3 months
Subsequent offence: Fine Rs. 4,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both
Note: Third-party insurance is mandatory for all vehicles.

Section 197 - Taking vehicle without authority
Fine: Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months, or both

Section 198 - Unauthorised interference with vehicle
Fine: Rs. 500

Section 199 - Offences by juveniles
Guardian/owner of vehicle liable: Fine Rs. 25,000 and imprisonment up to 3 months
Vehicle registration cancelled. Juvenile not to be tried under MV Act but under JJ Act.

Section 199A - Offences by juveniles (enhanced)
Guardian/owner: Fine Rs. 25,000 and 3 years imprisonment
Vehicle registration to be cancelled.

=== PART II: TRAFFIC SIGNAL VIOLATIONS ===

Red Light Jumping (Signal Violation):
Fine: Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000 depending on state
Under Section 177/184 MV Act
Note: Jumping red light is treated as dangerous driving in many states.

Wrong Side Driving:
Fine: Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 5,000
Treated as dangerous driving under Section 184.

No Parking Violation:
Fine: Rs. 500 to Rs. 1,000 plus towing charges
Vehicle can be towed and impounded.

Triple Riding on Two-Wheeler:
Fine: Rs. 1,000 under Section 194A

Driving without PUC (Pollution Under Control Certificate):
Fine: Rs. 1,000 (first offence), Rs. 2,000 (subsequent offence) under Section 190

=== PART III: DOCUMENTS REQUIRED WHILE DRIVING ===

Mandatory documents every driver must carry:
1. Driving Licence (DL) - Section 3 MV Act
2. Vehicle Registration Certificate (RC) - Section 39 MV Act
3. Insurance Certificate (valid third-party insurance) - Section 146 MV Act
4. Pollution Under Control (PUC) Certificate - Section 56 MV Act
5. Fitness Certificate (for commercial vehicles) - Section 56 MV Act
6. Permit (for commercial/transport vehicles) - Section 66 MV Act

Penalty for not carrying documents:
- No DL: Fine Rs. 5,000 (Section 181)
- No RC: Fine Rs. 5,000 (Section 192)
- No Insurance: Fine Rs. 2,000 (Section 196)
- No PUC: Fine Rs. 1,000 (Section 190)

=== PART IV: RIGHTS OF VEHICLE OWNER DURING POLICE STOP ===

Your Rights when stopped by Traffic Police:
1. Police must show their identity card if asked.
2. Police can only check documents listed above - they CANNOT demand money without issuing a proper challan.
3. You have the right to receive a proper printed/e-challan receipt for any fine.
4. Police CANNOT seize your vehicle for minor offences like no helmet or no seat belt.
5. Vehicle can be seized only for: no registration, no insurance, driving under influence, or court orders.
6. You can pay challan online at echallan.parivahan.gov.in
7. If police misbehave or demand bribe: Note badge number, file complaint at police station or call 100.
8. You can contest a challan in court within 60 days.

=== PART V: VEHICLE IMPOUNDMENT RULES ===

Section 207 - Detention of vehicles used without certificate of registration, etc.
A police officer in uniform or an officer of the Motor Vehicles Department may detain a vehicle if:
- Vehicle has no valid registration
- Vehicle has no valid insurance
- Vehicle is being driven by a person without a valid licence
- Vehicle is overloaded
- Vehicle is being used in a manner dangerous to public

Procedure after impoundment:
1. Police must give a receipt/acknowledgment of seized vehicle.
2. Owner must be informed of the reason for seizure.
3. Vehicle released after payment of fine and production of valid documents.
4. If vehicle not claimed within 30 days, it may be auctioned.

=== PART VI: ACCIDENT LAWS ===

Section 134 - Duty of driver in case of accident
After an accident, the driver MUST:
1. Stop the vehicle immediately.
2. Provide assistance to injured persons.
3. Take injured to nearest hospital.
4. Report the accident to nearest police station within 24 hours.
5. Give name, address, insurance details to police.

Failure to report accident: Fine Rs. 5,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months (Section 187)
Hit and run: Compensation of Rs. 2,00,000 for death, Rs. 50,000 for grievous hurt (Section 161)

Section 140 - Liability to pay compensation in certain cases on the principle of no fault
In case of death: Compensation of Rs. 5,00,000
In case of permanent disablement: Compensation of Rs. 2,50,000
This is payable regardless of fault (no-fault liability).

=== PART VII: DRIVING LICENCE RULES ===

Section 3 - Necessity for driving licence
No person shall drive a motor vehicle in any public place unless he holds an effective driving licence.

Learner's Licence:
- Minimum age: 16 years for motorcycles up to 50cc, 18 years for others
- Valid for 6 months
- Must display 'L' board on vehicle
- Must be accompanied by person with valid DL

Permanent Driving Licence:
- Can apply after 30 days of holding Learner's Licence
- Minimum age: 18 years (20 years for transport vehicles)
- Valid for 20 years or till age 40, whichever is earlier; then renewed every 5 years

Disqualification from holding DL:
- Drunk driving: 6 months to 2 years
- Dangerous driving causing death: 2 years minimum
- Racing on public roads: 1 month to 1 year

=== PART VIII: INSURANCE RULES ===

Section 146 - Necessity for insurance against third party risk
No person shall use a motor vehicle in a public place unless there is in force a policy of insurance complying with the requirements of this Chapter.

Third-party insurance is MANDATORY. Comprehensive insurance is optional.

Penalty for no insurance: Fine Rs. 2,000 or imprisonment up to 3 months (first offence)

=== PART IX: CHALLAN PROCESS ===

E-Challan System:
- Traffic police issue e-challans through handheld devices.
- Challan is sent to registered mobile number and email.
- Can be paid online at: echallan.parivahan.gov.in
- Can also be paid at nearest traffic police station.
- Unpaid challans can result in vehicle registration renewal being blocked.

Contesting a Challan:
- You can contest a challan in the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal or Traffic Court.
- Appear on the date mentioned in the challan.
- If you believe the challan is wrong, you can file a written objection.

=== PART X: SPECIAL PROVISIONS ===

School Zone / Hospital Zone:
- Speed limit: 25 km/h
- Penalty for violation: Double the normal fine

Highway Speed Limits:
- Cars/Jeeps: 100 km/h on national highways, 70 km/h on state highways
- Two-wheelers: 80 km/h on national highways
- Trucks/Buses: 80 km/h on national highways
- In cities: 50 km/h general limit, 30 km/h in residential areas

Drunk Driving (DUI) Procedure:
1. Police can stop any vehicle and conduct breathalyzer test.
2. If BAC exceeds 30mg/100ml, driver is arrested.
3. Vehicle is impounded.
4. FIR registered under Section 185 MV Act.
5. Driver produced before Magistrate within 24 hours.
6. Bail may be granted by Magistrate.
7. Licence suspended pending trial.
